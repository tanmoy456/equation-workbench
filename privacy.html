Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 18, 2026 · Last updated: June 18, 2026

This policy explains what the Equation Workbench web editor and Google Slides add-on (together, the “Service”) access and how that information is handled. The Service is provided by gtanmoy (the “Developer”). By using the Service you agree to this policy.

The short version. The Service runs in your browser. It does not collect, transmit, sell, or store your personal information on any server operated by the Developer. The Slides add-on only reads and edits the presentation you have open, and only when you actively use it.

1. The web editor

The web editor at this site processes everything locally in your browser:

  • The LaTeX you type is rendered into an image in your browser. It is not sent to the Developer or any third party for processing.
  • Equations you choose to save to the editor’s library are stored locally on your device/browser so you can reload them. They are not uploaded to any server controlled by the Developer.
  • Files you export (SVG, PNG) and clipboard copies are produced on your device and never transmitted to the Developer.

2. The Google Slides add-on

When you use the add-on, it requests permission to work with your Google Slides presentations. Specifically:

  • What it accesses: the content of the Google Slides presentation that is currently open, so it can insert an equation image, read an equation’s stored source, and replace an equation in place.
  • When it accesses it: only while the add-on is open and you trigger an action (insert, read, or replace). It does not run in the background or scan other files in your Google Drive.
  • What it stores: the LaTeX source of an inserted equation is saved inside that image’s alternative-text (description) field within your own presentation. This is what allows you to edit the equation later. This data lives only in your presentation, under your control.
  • What leaves your device: nothing is sent to the Developer. Equation rendering happens in your browser.

3. Google user data and limited use

The Service’s use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. The Service does not transfer, sell, or use Google user data for advertising, and accesses only the data needed to provide the equation-editing features you request.

4. Third-party services

To render equations, the Service loads the open-source MathJax typesetting library from a public content delivery network (Cloudflare’s cdnjs). This is a standard request for a static JavaScript file; the LaTeX you type is not sent to that CDN. Your browser’s normal request to the CDN may be logged by the CDN provider under its own policies.

5. Data retention and deletion

The Developer does not retain your data because the Developer does not collect it. Equations saved in the web editor’s library can be deleted by you within the editor or by clearing your browser storage. LaTeX stored in a slide’s alt text is removed when you delete or replace that equation, or edit the alt-text field in Slides.

6. Children’s privacy

The Service is a general-purpose productivity tool and is not directed at children under 13.

7. Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the “Last updated” date above.
